# Strategic Pricing Exceptions

Last revised: September 29, 2025
Owner: Revenue Leadership

## Why this exists

Standard policy protects margin, but high-risk renewals sometimes justify exceptions that would be unreasonable as a default rule.

## Strategic retention exception

For accounts above $40k in annual contract value, leadership may approve a retention exception when all three conditions are true:

1. There is documented churn or renewal risk.
2. The customer experienced a meaningful service failure or account-management miss.
3. The account has expansion or reference value beyond the current contract.

## Allowed exception range

- Preferred remedy: service credit, not cash refund.
- Typical ceiling: one billing cycle.
- Stretch ceiling with CEO approval: up to two billing cycles.

## Example account shape

TeraCorp, at $48k/year, is the kind of account that can qualify for a stretch exception if the commercial risk is real and leadership agrees the standard policy is too rigid for the moment.

## Important caveat

This memo exists to describe exceptions, not to replace the refund policy. In practice, teams still disagree on whether finance must approve before the CEO or simply be informed before the customer reply goes out.
