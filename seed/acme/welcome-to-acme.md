# Welcome to Acme

This note is the quick-start packet new operators get on week one. It is intentionally practical, lightly opinionated, and a little out of date in places because the business keeps moving faster than the handbook.

## Who does what

- Marcus Nwosu owns customer success escalations for enterprise accounts.
- Priya Raman runs support operations and gets pulled into incidents that risk churn.
- Femi Adebayo owns finance approvals for credits, refunds, and non-standard commercial terms.
- Jordan Mee makes the final call on strategic exceptions when retention is at stake.

## Enterprise accounts to know

- TeraCorp: $48k/year, high-touch, quick to escalate when product reliability slips.
- BrightHarbor: $36k/year, detail-oriented buyer, security-heavy procurement motions.
- Northline: $22k/year, referenceable logo, sensitive to invoice timing.

## Escalation norms

- If an outage hits a customer above $40k ARR, loop Priya in early.
- If the account is strategically important, bring Jordan in before the customer goes cold.
- For credits above one month, the shortcut version is: tell finance, then get Jordan aligned.

That last bullet is the hallway version, not the full policy. The formal docs are stricter.

## How Acme talks to customers

We do not debate in front of the customer. Marcus gathers facts, Priya pressure-tests the incident story, Femi checks the commercial reality, and Jordan only steps in when the company needs a single clear answer.
